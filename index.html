Part 0: Overview

For this project, our team created a user interface for a Y-Combinator startup named OnScale. OnScale seeks to connect creators with brands so that the creators can spend more time creating. With this premise in mind, our team created concepts for webpages, then wireframes for a possible app, and finally, a high fidelity mock up to test with real users.

Part 1: Sketching and Wireframing

OnScale is a Y-Combinator startup seeking to connect small creators to brands. We chose to make our design of OnScale on a desktop application. This decision is because creators, who this app is target towards, do most of their content creation such as editing, streaming, etc, on desktop computers.

To kick off our redesign of the app, we each completed our own LoFi sketch based on our personal understanding of OnScale to generate as many ideas as possible. In total, we ended up with four sketches to work off of:

Part 2: Mockups

Interactive HiFi Mockup

First, we created an interactive hifi mockup of our app design using the combined lofi design. Then, we presented our mockup to our class and an industry professional for feedback.

Feedback

We recieved the following feedback about our design:

  1. Make it clear how to go back to the previous page.
  2. Change the horizontal scrolling elements due to difficulties interacting with them when using a mouse.
  3. Provide more information about the brands when they are presented and tell the user why they were recommended.
  4. Add a timeline for partnerships to make it more clear which partnerships were current and which were from the past.

Our Fixes and Improved Design

We fixed the user feedback with the following changes:

  1. Fixed the issue with navigation between pages by adding a breadcrumb to the top of each subpage, allowing users to easily navigate back to the main page they came from.
  2. We replaced the horizontal scrolling elements with buttons, allowing users to simply click the buttons to make the element scroll rather than having to scroll themselves using a trackpad.
  3. For the missing brand information, we had a couple of solutions:
    • We added a small piece of text below each recommended brand to give intuition as to why they were recommended to that particular creator.
    • We added a TLDR for each partnered brand on the profile page in order to give more information about the partnership without requiring the user to read a lot.
  4. For the critiques where users wanted a more intuitive understanding of the current partners vs. the past partners, we added two new elements to our prototype:
    • We gave the option to filter the partnerships by both current partnerships and past partnerships.
    • We added a timeline, giving a more visual representation of when each partnership took place.
